# SpaceBot

An interactive, AI-powered companion for exploring Mars, NASA's missions, and the wider universe.

SpaceBot brings together conversational AI, real-time 3D visualisation, and NASA's open data into a single playground for learners, enthusiasts, and the merely curious.

## Features

### Conversational AI assistant
An integrated chatbot powered by a Large Language Model (LLM). Ask anything about Mars geology, mission timelines, orbital mechanics, or what a particular instrument on a rover actually does — SpaceBot answers in plain language and can dig deeper on request.

### Interactive 3D view of Mars and space
A fully rotatable, zoomable 3D scene of Mars and its surrounding space. Switch between viewing points — orbital, surface, satellite-eye — to inspect features at different scales. The view is built for exploration, not just decoration: pan around, lock onto landmarks, and move through the scene at your own pace.

### NASA rocket playground
A live, visual playground that recreates how NASA's rockets actually fly. Watch staging, trajectory shaping, and burn profiles play out in a simplified physical model, and compare how different launch vehicles climb out of the atmosphere and reach their targets.

### Mars-focused video and satellite imagery feed
A curated stream of NASA's official videos and satellite imagery, with a deliberate focus on Mars. New content is pulled from NASA's public sources so the feed stays current with active missions, rover photography, and orbital observations of the Martian surface.

### Labeled diagrams of NASA's tools and equipment
On demand, SpaceBot generates labeled diagrams of NASA's hardware — rockets, rovers, landers, and scientific instruments. Each diagram annotates the major components so you can see *what* a piece of equipment is made of and *how* its parts work together.

## Status

This project is in early development. Features above describe the intended scope, not the current shipped state.
